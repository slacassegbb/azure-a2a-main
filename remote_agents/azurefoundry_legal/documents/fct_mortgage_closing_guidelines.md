# FCT Mortgage Closing Guidelines — Canadian Residential Real Estate

_Reference: FCT Lender Lawyer Connect (LLC) Standard Procedures | Version 1.0_

---

## 1. Overview

FCT (First Canadian Title) acts as the intermediary between lenders and lawyers in residential mortgage transactions across Canada. This document outlines the standard closing conditions, legal requirements, and escalation triggers for title insurance-backed mortgage closings.

---

## 2. Standard Closing Conditions

All of the following conditions must be satisfied before closing instructions are issued to the borrower's lawyer:

### 2.1 Identity & Borrower Verification
- Government-issued photo ID confirmed for all borrowers on title
- Identity matches mortgage application — name, date of birth, SIN (where applicable)
- No AML (Anti-Money Laundering) flags on borrower file
- Client ID verification completed per FINTRAC requirements

### 2.2 Title & Property
- Title search completed — no undisclosed liens, encumbrances, or caveats
- Property matches the legal description on the mortgage commitment
- Existing mortgage(s) to be discharged confirmed with payout statement
- Property tax account verified — no outstanding arrears
- Zoning confirmed as consistent with intended use (residential)

### 2.3 Insurance
- Title insurance policy bound with FCT prior to closing
- Homeowner's/fire insurance confirmed with lender named as loss payee
- Coverage amount meets or exceeds replacement value of the property

### 2.4 Mortgage Documentation
- Signed mortgage commitment received from lender
- Mortgage instructions issued and acknowledged by borrower's lawyer
- All conditions on the commitment letter resolved and documented
- Mortgage registration amount matches commitment exactly

### 2.5 Payout & Discharge (Refinance / Transfer)
- Payout statement received from existing lender(s)
- Discharge of prior mortgage confirmed via FCT Payout service
- Bridge financing (if applicable) documented and approved

---

## 3. Closing Instruction Letter — Required Elements

When all conditions are satisfied, the closing instruction letter issued to the lawyer must include:

1. **Transaction reference number** (FCT file number)
2. **Property address** and legal description
3. **Borrower name(s)** as they appear on title
4. **Lender name** and mortgage amount
5. **Closing date and time** (funding deadline)
6. **List of satisfied conditions** with confirmation checkmarks
7. **Outstanding items** (if any) with resolution deadline
8. **Payout amounts** for any discharges required on closing
9. **Wire transfer instructions** for mortgage proceeds
10. **Lawyer certification requirement** — lawyer must confirm title is clear before funds release

---

## 4. Risk Flags — Conditions Requiring Escalation

The following situations require escalation to a senior FCT reviewer before closing instructions are issued:

| Flag | Reason |
|---|---|
| Title has undischarged mortgage from previous owner | Possible title defect |
| Borrower name mismatch between ID and title | Potential identity issue |
| Property tax arrears > $2,000 | Lien risk |
| Closing date within 24 hours with incomplete conditions | Rush closing risk |
| Payout amount differs from commitment by > $500 | Potential fraud indicator |
| Property transferred within last 90 days | Rapid resale / title flipping risk |
| Multiple mortgages being discharged simultaneously | Complexity / coordination risk |
| Borrower has prior title insurance claim on record | Repeat claim risk |

## 5. Provincial Considerations

| Province | Key Requirement |
|---|---|
| Ontario | Electronic registration via Teraview; lawyer must be registered user |
| British Columbia | Land Title Act compliance; Property Transfer Tax confirmed |
| Alberta | Land Titles Act; SPIN2 search required |
| Quebec | Notary (not lawyer) required; civil law system applies |
| Other provinces | Common law applies; standard FCT LLC process |

---

## 6. Timelines

- **Standard closing**: Conditions must be satisfied 48 hours before closing date
- **Rush closing**: Same-day closing requires senior FCT approval and all conditions pre-cleared
- **Payout discharge**: Typically processed within 3–5 business days post-closing
- **Title insurance policy**: Issued at closing, effective from registration date

---

## 7. Lawyer Confirmation Requirements

Before funds are released by the lender, the borrower's lawyer must certify:
- Title is clear and marketable
- All FCT conditions have been met
- Mortgage documents have been signed by all borrowers
- Property insurance is in place
- Closing proceeds will be applied as directed in the instruction letter

---

## 8. Common Closing Failures

- **Missing discharge**: Prior mortgage payout not received in time — delay closing
- **ID mismatch**: Borrower ID does not match title exactly — escalate immediately
- **Insurance lapse**: Homeowner insurance expired or not confirmed — cannot fund
- **Tax arrears discovered late**: Requires holdback or payment from proceeds
- **Wrong mortgage amount**: Commitment and registration amount differ — stop funding, contact lender
