# Universal Claims Reference

_Last updated: 2025-09-24 | Version: 1.0_

---

## 1. Intake Checklist

1. Verify identity and policy details.  
2. Record date, time, and cause of loss.  
3. Confirm active coverage and endorsements.  
4. Collect necessary documents (photos, reports, receipts).  
5. Identify third parties involved (adverse drivers, landlords, witnesses).  
6. Check for fraud indicators.  
7. Communicate next steps and SLA to customer.  

---

## 2. Service Level Agreements (SLA)

- Claim acknowledgement: within 1 business day.  
- Status updates: every 72 hours.  
- Estimate review: within 5 business days.  
- Payment issuance: within 3 business days of settlement.  
- Escalation: unresolved >30 days → team lead; >45 days → compliance.  

---

## 3. Fraud Red Flags

- Loss reported more than 30 days late.  
- 2+ prior similar claims in past 18 months.  
- Story inconsistencies between insured and witnesses.  
- Suspicious timing (policy recently increased, loss soon after).  
- Stolen vehicle with no second key or GPS disabled.  
- Fire shortly after new policy bound.  

---

## 4. Communication Standards

- Always empathetic and factual.  
- Avoid promising coverage before review.  
- Cite policy sections when approving/denying.  
- Provide clear next steps and timelines.  
- Keep records in file for compliance audits.
