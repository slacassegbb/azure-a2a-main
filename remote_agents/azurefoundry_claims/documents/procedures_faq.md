# Claims Processing Procedures & FAQs

_Last updated: 2025-09-24 | Version: 1.0_

---

## 1. End-to-End Workflow

1. **First Notice of Loss (FNOL):** Log claim, assign adjuster.  
2. **Coverage Check:** Validate active policy, deductibles, endorsements.  
3. **Document Request:** Photos, reports, estimates, receipts.  
4. **Investigation:** Interview insured, witnesses, third parties.  
5. **Estimate Review:** Validate repair/replacement costs.  
6. **Decision:** Approve, partially approve, or deny.  
7. **Payment:** Issue within 3 business days of agreement.  
8. **Closure:** Confirm insured has received payment and understands outcome.  

---

## 2. Escalation Rules

- Claims unresolved after 30 days → team lead review.  
- Claims unresolved after 45 days → compliance/legal review.  
- 2+ fraud indicators → Special Investigations Unit (SIU).  
- Payment >$50k → mandatory manager approval.  

---

## 3. Compliance Guidance

- Document every decision and cite policy language.  
- Ensure payments match documented damages and limits.  
- Provide appeal process in denial letters.  
- Adhere to privacy standards: redact unnecessary PII.  
- Bad faith exposure avoided by timely communication and fair handling.  

---

## 4. Frequently Asked Questions

### General
- **How long does it take to settle a claim?**  
  Most claims close within 15–30 days depending on documentation.  

- **What if I disagree with a decision?**  
  Submit a written appeal; senior adjuster will re-review.  

- **When will I get my payment?**  
  Within 3 business days of settlement.  

### Auto
- **Can I keep my salvage vehicle?**  
  Yes, but payout reduced by salvage value.  

- **Will rental car be covered if my car just broke down?**  
  No, only covered if related to a covered collision or comprehensive loss.  

### Home
- **Is flood covered?**  
  No, unless you have a separate flood policy.  

- **Are high-value items like jewelry fully covered?**  
  No, capped at $5k unless specifically scheduled.  

### Travel
- **Does my policy cover pre-existing conditions?**  
  No, unless specifically endorsed.  

- **What happens if my bag is delayed 12 hours?**  
  Not covered; baggage delay requires at least 24 hours delay.
