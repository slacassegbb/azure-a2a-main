# Health Fraud Detection Reference

_Last updated: 2025-09-24 | Version 3.0_

---

## 1. Common Fraud Indicators
- Billing for services not rendered.  
- Upcoding to higher-cost codes.  
- Duplicate billing.  
- Cosmetic surgery disguised as emergency.  
- Prescription refills unusually frequent.  

---

## 2. Known Schemes
- **Phantom Billing:** Dozens of identical claims same day.  
- **Drug Diversion:** Medications billed, resold.  
- **Kickbacks:** Provider + patient share proceeds.  
- **Injury Misclassification:** Elective procedure coded as accident.  

---

## 3. Escalation Triggers
- Unusually high claim volume from one provider.  
- Out-of-network billing at inflated rates.  
- Inconsistent medical documentation.  

---

## 4. Case Studies
- **Fraudulent – Phantom MRI Billing:**  
  - Provider billed 15 MRIs in one day.  
  - Identical reports across patients.  
  - → Escalated, provider flagged.  

- **Legitimate – Appendectomy:**  
  - ER records + surgery + 3-day stay.  
  - Costs matched procedure ($28,500).  
  - → Paid.
