# Travel Fraud Detection Reference

_Last updated: 2025-09-24 | Version 3.0_

---

## 1. Common Fraud Indicators
- Cancellation unsupported by documents.  
- Forged medical certificates.  
- High-value baggage claims with no proof.  
- Duplicate receipts reused.  
- Trips booked, cancelled immediately.  

---

## 2. Known Schemes
- **Baggage Padding:** Adding luxury items to claims.  
- **Cancelled But Traveled:** Insured travels but claims refund.  
- **Receipt Inflation:** Fake hotel/meal bills.  
- **Phantom Traveler:** Fake family members on itinerary.  

---

## 3. Escalation Triggers
- Baggage loss >$5k with no receipts.  
- Cancellation with no airline/hotel proof.  
- Multiple travelers file identical claims.  

---

## 4. Case Studies
- **Fraudulent – Inflated Baggage Claim:**  
  - Claimed $12k in jewelry + electronics.  
  - No receipts, bag weighed 18kg.  
  - Social posts show items after trip.  
  - → Denied, escalated.  

- **Legitimate – Airline Strike:**  
  - Airline cancellation notice provided.  
  - Prepaid trip $3,800.  
  - → Paid in full.
